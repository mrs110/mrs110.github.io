Profile

IBM Service Hub

The IBM Service Hub is a platform connecting insurance companies, their clients and health service providers. Over 70% of Germany’s private health insurers use services from the hub’s portfolio.

As a mobile consultant I am the interface between our clients and the product team. I identify features, together with our clients, and discuss these with our development and product management teams before placing them on the product roadmap. I have led engineering teams to implement features and delivered over six apps to our clients.

As a former member of the development team I prototyped our test automation to cut our time to market and to increase code quality. I have also modernized our tooling, introducing Git and Slack, to improve our collaboration.

The screenshot shows the IBM Bluepostbox, one of the Service Hub’s mobile products. I developed parts of the app and led the introduction of the android app at our first client (Gothaer Lebensversicherung).

Let's Talk

Let’sTalk is a startup based in Santiago de Chile building an instant messaging solution to connect service providers with customers.

I was Let’s Talk’s second engineering hire and prepared the web app for customer service operators for the launch, identifying, designing and implementing user-centric features (see screenshot of the web app). After the launch I received direct feedback from our users on newly introduced features. I used the feedback to tweak existing and find new features.

I learned a lot from working closely with the CTO on a day to day basis. We discussed the company’s privacy policy and overall strategy.

Dialog Semiconductor

Dialog Semiconductor designs power management microchips for mobile phones. Developing these chips needs extensive testing, which, due to the nature of batteries, is very time consuming.

I build a tool for engineers to automate the data capture process and to allow live data analysis. I was the product manager, product owner and development team on this project and went through several Build-Measure-Learn cycles, collecting user feedback and improving the product iteratively.

As of January 2017 the tool is still in use, showing that I have succeeded in building a meaningful product.
